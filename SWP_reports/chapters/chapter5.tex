\section{Business Rules}

The following business rules define the constraints and policies applied throughout the Personalized Career Orientation \& Learning Roadmap Platform.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Business Rules}
\begin{tabular}{|c|p{11cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{ID} & \textbf{Rule Definition} \\ \hline

BR-01 &
A student can own only one profile in the system.
\\ \hline

BR-02 &
A student can select only one target career role at a time.
\\ \hline

BR-03 &
A personalized roadmap can only be generated after a target career role has been selected.
\\ \hline

BR-04 &
Each Skill Node belongs to exactly one Tech Path.
\\ \hline

BR-05 &
A Skill Node cannot be marked as completed unless it exists in the student's roadmap.
\\ \hline

BR-06 &
Roadmap progress is automatically recalculated whenever the completion status changes.
\\ \hline

BR-07 &
AI recommendations are generated using the student's transcript, GitHub repositories, and selected career role.
\\ \hline

BR-08 &
A student may upload multiple transcripts, but only the latest version is used for analysis.
\\ \hline

BR-09 &
One GitHub account can only be linked to one system account.
\\ \hline

BR-10 &
Only public GitHub repositories are synchronized.
\\ \hline

BR-11 &
Repositories without a README.md file are not analyzed by the AI.
\\ \hline

BR-12 &
Each repository is synchronized only once during a synchronization cycle.
\\ \hline

BR-13 &
One AI-generated summary is created for every synchronized repository.
\\ \hline

BR-14 &
Skill Gap Analysis compares only the skills required for the selected career role.
\\ \hline

BR-15 &
Missing skills are prioritized according to industry demand and prerequisite relationships.
\\ \hline

BR-16 &
Generated Skill Gap reports are stored for future reference.
\\ \hline

BR-17 &
Market Pulse data is automatically updated every 24 hours.
\\ \hline

BR-18 &
Only verified job postings are included in technology trend analysis.
\\ \hline

BR-19 &
Technology popularity is calculated using keyword frequency extracted from job descriptions.
\\ \hline

BR-20 &
All AI mentor conversations are stored in the user's chat history.
\\ \hline

BR-21 &
Users may delete their chat history without affecting roadmap data.
\\ \hline

BR-22 &
Each student owns exactly one shareable E-Portfolio URL.
\\ \hline

BR-23 &
Generating a new E-Portfolio URL invalidates the previous one.
\\ \hline

BR-24 &
Only authenticated users can access personalized recommendations.
\\ \hline

BR-25 &
Google OAuth users must authenticate with a verified Google account.
\\ \hline

\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\section{Assumptions and Dependencies}

This section describes the assumptions made during system development and the external dependencies required for system operation.

\subsection{Assumptions}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Users have a stable Internet connection.
    \item GitHub repositories are publicly accessible.
    \item Uploaded academic transcripts are valid and readable.
    \item AI services (OpenAI or Gemini) are available during recommendation requests.
    \item External job portals provide accessible data for technology trend analysis.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Dependencies}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{External Dependencies}
\begin{tabular}{|l|p{9cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{Service} & \textbf{Purpose} \\ \hline

OpenAI API / Gemini API &
Generate AI career recommendations and chatbot responses.
\\ \hline

GitHub REST API &
Synchronize repositories and retrieve README files.
\\ \hline

Google OAuth 2.0 &
User authentication.
\\ \hline

Job Portal APIs &
Collect job market information and technology trends.
\\ \hline

PDF Generator &
Export Skill Gap Analysis reports.
\\ \hline

\end{tabular}
\end{table}
